There are three layers to a tooth; the enamel, the dentin, and the nerve chamber.

  • Enamel

    The enamel is the outermost layer of the tooth which is porous, similar to a sponge.

  • Dentin

    These pores in the enamel are the start of microscopic tunnels called dentinal tubules.

  • Nerve Chamber

    The dentinal tubules which lead to the inner layers of the tooth called the dentin ending is at the nerve chamber.
