It’s important to explain the anatomy of a tooth to properly understand how teeth can change color and with that, potentially experience sensitivity.

Tooth Anatomy Glossary
  • Enamel - the top, porous white outer part of the tooth mostly composed of a rock-hard mineral called calcium phosphate.
  • Dentin - the layer under the enamel, it is composed of a hard tissue and dentinal tubules.
  • Pulp - the innermost living structure of the tooth where blood vessels and the nerve of the tooth lives.
UP Close
Teeth are porous, similar to our skin. The pores of the enamel are connected to the dentin where dentinal tubules are. Dentinal tubules are tiny tubules that run from the inside of the tooth (the pulp chamber) out through the hard dentin and end beneath the enamel.
Even Closer

Dentinal tubules are unique to each person, they vary in shape and size. This is mostly due to genetics and diet, it is a fact that having a more acidic diet decomposes smear plugs and enlarges the inner diameter of the dentinal tubule.

Ideally, dentinal tubules have naturally-formed smear plugs blocking or plugging the entrance to the tubules, when dentinal tubule smear plugs are lost it results in potential for dental sensitivity.
