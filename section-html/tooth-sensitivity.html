There are two distinct types of tooth sensitivity

1
Typical Dentinal Hypersensitivity

A generalized discomfort of the teeth where the dental pulp overreacts to various stimuli such as cold temperatures and the simple act of brushing your teeth. Sometimes associated with genuine pathologic conditions such as caries, occlusal trauma, cracks in teeth, or leakage under faulty restorations.

2
Zingers

Zingers are those instantaneous sharp zings that shoot down the length of your teeth feeling like an electric shock! Thankfully they end as soon as they begin but they are most definitely intimidating.
